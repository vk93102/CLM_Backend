1. DOCUMENT CONTROL & PURPOSE
1.1 Purpose
This Legal Compliance Management Template provides a structured framework for in-house legal teams to identify, track, monitor, and report on compliance obligations under applicable United States federal and state laws and regulations. It is intended to serve as a living document updated on a regular basis by the Legal Department.
1.2 Scope
This template applies to all business units, subsidiaries, and affiliates of the organization operating within the United States. It covers:
	•	Federal statutory and regulatory requirements (e.g., ADA, FCPA, SEC, OSHA, HIPAA, GDPR cross-border)
	•	State-specific legal requirements in all jurisdictions where the organization operates
	•	Industry-specific regulatory mandates applicable to the organization's sector
	•	Contractual compliance obligations with legal or regulatory implications

5.1 Reporting Obligations Checklist
Upon identifying a potential violation, verify mandatory reporting requirements:
	•	Determine whether a federal or state mandatory disclosure obligation is triggered (e.g., data breach — notify affected individuals within state-mandated timeframe)
	•	Consult with General Counsel and outside counsel as necessary
	•	Preserve all relevant records under a legal hold
	•	Document all remediation steps taken with dates and responsible parties
	•	Notify insurer if D&O, E&O, or cyber liability coverage may apply

9.2 External Reporting Obligations
Certain violations require mandatory disclosure to government agencies. Failure to comply may constitute an independent violation. Key mandatory reporting scenarios include:
	•	Data breach affecting 500+ individuals: Notify HHS/OCR within 60 days (HIPAA); notify affected individuals per applicable state law (varies 30–90 days)
	•	Securities violations: Report to SEC via Form 8-K within 4 business days of materiality determination
	•	Workplace injuries resulting in hospitalization: Notify OSHA within 24 hours (serious injuries) or 8 hours (fatalities)
	•	Suspected FCPA violation: Consult external counsel immediately; voluntary disclosure to DOJ/SEC may be appropriate
	•	Sanctions violations: Notify OFAC; no voluntary exception — contact outside counsel immediately
